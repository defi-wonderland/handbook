# Communication

We use two main tools to stay in sync: **Discord** for internal coordination, and **Slack** for partner communication. Each has a clear purpose, and together they cover everything from project updates to day-to-day discussions.

## Discord: internal comms

Discord is where we collaborate across teams, share updates, and hold internal meetings.

You’ll use it for:
- Async discussions and announcements
- Voice and video calls
- Topic-specific channels and threads

When you join:
- You’ll be assigned one or more **roles** based on your function (e.g. Dev, Lead, Ops)
- Roles define which channels you can see
- You can remove roles if your focus changes

**Threads** are used to keep things focused. Inside each channel, create a thread for any new discussion topic rather than replying inline. This keeps conversations organized and easy to find later.

> 💡 New topic? Start a new thread instead of replying to an old one.

## Slack: partner comms

We use Slack to communicate with partners who prefer it. It’s the external-facing equivalent of Discord. You’ll only be added to channels that relate to the partners or teams you’re working with. This keeps the noise down and helps you focus.

## Communication guidelines

- **No DMs to partners** unless absolutely necessary  
  Use shared channels so others can see decisions and follow-ups.

- **Keep it clear and professional**  
  Friendly is good. Casual is fine. But always confirm changes or agreements in the channel.

- **Use threads**  
  Keeps conversations structured and easier to catch up on.

- **Tag with intention**  
  Use mentions to bring something to someone's attention, but don’t overdo it.

- **React instead of replying**  
  Use emojis to acknowledge or confirm something when a message doesn’t need a response.

# Core Tools

We use a few main tools to manage work and keep projects moving. These help us plan, assign tasks, review progress, and track availability. This section covers how we organize work and time at Wonderland.

## Planning & Tracking

### Linear: projects and tasks

We use **Linear** to manage projects, track issues, and organize all the work that goes into delivering for partners. It helps us stay aligned on priorities, timelines, and team responsibilities.

We work with partners long-term, often across multiple projects. In Linear, that’s reflected in our structure:

```
Team → Project → Epic → Task
```

- **Team** = The partner we’re working with  
- **Project** = A product or initiative under that partner  
- **Epic** = A feature or workstream within the project  
- **Task** = A unit of work with clear scope and ownership  

You’ll only see the Teams you’re actively involved in.

#### Epics

Epics group related tasks and help us see the bigger picture. A good way to structure them:

- Start by writing out the key tasks
- Group those by function or outcome into epics

For example, if you’re working on smart contracts:
- Each contract can be an epic
- Inside: tasks for writing, testing, audit, documentation, bug fixing

This makes it easier to plan and track work as it evolves.

#### Labels

We tag tasks with labels to clarify what type of work they involve. This gives visibility into project patterns and helps with planning.

Example:
If a project has a lot of tasks labeled *“optimization”*, but no one with that focus is staffed, we might need to bring in support or adjust scope.

#### Estimates

We use story points to estimate how much time a task will take. This helps with pacing and planning. Here's the scale we use:

| Story Points | Estimated Time     |
|--------------|--------------------|
| 0            | Up to 1 hour       |
| 1            | 3–4 hours          |
| 2            | 1–1.5 days         |
| 3            | 2.5 days           |
| 5            | 3.5 days           |
| 8            | 4–5 days           |
| 13           | 1–2 weeks          |
| 21           | More than 2 weeks  |

:::warning
Tasks with 5+ points should usually be split up. Breaking work into smaller chunks makes it easier to track and review.
:::

#### Why we use linear

- Keeps project status visible to everyone
- Helps us plan realistically and adjust scope when needed
- Reinforces ownership and accountability across the team


### Float: scheduling and availability

**Float** is where we track availability and plan workloads. It shows who’s working on what, when, and for how long, across all teams and projects.

Each person’s calendar shows their assignments, workload, time off, and role in each project (e.g. Dev, Lead, Researcher).

You’ll also see public holidays (if requested) and OOO blocks. If something looks off or your schedule is empty, ping the COO.

#### Going Out of Office (OOO)

When planning time off:

1. Fill out the [OOO request form on Notion](https://www.notion.so/e8af51864bbf4b7a9dfd77cbde81e2c8?pvs=21)
2. Block the same dates in Float and label them as “Out of Office”
3. Add a link or reference to your Notion request
4. Discuss it with your Partner Lead
5. They’ll raise it with the COO on their weekly call

Don’t book anything until the time off is approved.

#### Sick leave

If you’re feeling unwell:
- Let your Partner Lead know right away
- They’ll inform the team and log it in Float

#### Travel or time zone changes

If you’re travelling or shifting time zones:
- Let your Partner Lead know
- Update your name in Discord to reflect your new hours

#### Public Holidays

Public holidays aren’t added automatically. If you want to take a public holiday off, just follow the OOO steps above.

### Why we use float

- Makes workload distribution more balanced
- Reduces scheduling issues and last-minute surprises
- Keeps the whole team aligned on who’s around and when
